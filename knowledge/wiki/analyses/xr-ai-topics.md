# XR and AI Research Topics

This page details the five main research topics identified in the scoping review of XR and AI intersection research [[XR-AI scoping review]](../sources/XR-AI.md).

## Topic Distribution
Based on 311 reviewed papers from 2017-2021:

| Topic | Percentage | Description |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| Using AI to create XR worlds | 28.6% | Content generation, environments, avatars |
| Using AI to understand users | 19.3% | User prediction, emotion, presence |
| Using AI to support interaction | 15.4% | Gestures, locomotion, devices |
| Investigating IVAs | 8.0% | Agent perception, empathy, trust |
| Using XR to Support AI Research | 2.3% | Visualization, training data |
| External applications | 23.8% | Medical, simulation, etc. |

## 1. Using AI to Create XR Worlds (28.6%)

### Three Approaches
1. **Realistically replicating real-world environments and human representations**
   - Focus on physical appearance and behavioral modeling
   - Most concentrated area of research

2. **Modifying real-world content for virtual contexts**
   - Adapting existing content for XR use

3. **Generating entirely synthetic virtual worlds**
   - Surprisingly limited generative approaches
   - Lack of GAN-based content creation despite potential

### Key Challenges
- Content creation challenges in VR remain significant
- Limited diversity in generated content

## 2. Using AI to Understand Users (19.3%)

### Primary Focus Areas
- **Predicting VR sickness** through various machine learning approaches
- **Predicting user characteristics**: emotion, presence
- **Analyzing viewport and head movement patterns**

### Limitations
- Research typically adopts a performance-driven perspective
- Limited examination of broader usability and user experience considerations
- Need for more comprehensive UX-focused research

## 3. Using AI to Support Interaction (15.4%)

### Key Areas
- **Gestural interaction**: improved hand tracking, pose estimation
- **Locomotion techniques**: particularly redirected walking
- **Novel interaction devices and techniques**

### Underrepresented Areas
- Haptic feedback research despite its importance in XR
- More diverse interaction modalities needed

## 4. Investigating Interaction with Intelligent Virtual Agents (8.0%)

### Research Focus
- User perception of agents through perceptual experiments
- Empathy modeling
- Trust assessment
- Personality modeling

### Critical Gap
- Agent behavior is typically scripted rather than genuinely implemented through AI models
- Need for more autonomous, AI-driven agent behaviors

## 5. Using XR to Support AI Research (2.3%)

### Applications
- **Visualizing AI methods** to improve understanding for non-experts
- Addressing limited training data challenges

### Significance
- Represents significant imbalance in research focus
- Could benefit from more attention

## Cross-Cutting Issues

### Dataset Limitations
- Limited dataset diversity and generalizability
- Small sample sizes common
- Bias toward young male Western populations

### Methodological Gaps
- Lack of theoretical and methodological guidelines for applying AI to specific XR problems
- Insufficient robustness testing across different tasks and populations

### Ethical Considerations
- Minimal discussion of ethical and societal implications
- Need for greater attention to societal impacts
