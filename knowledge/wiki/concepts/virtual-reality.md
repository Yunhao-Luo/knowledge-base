# Virtual Reality

## Definition
**Virtual Reality (VR)** is an immersive technology that creates simulated environments, typically experienced through head-mounted displays or other specialized hardware. It represents the most immersive form of Extended Reality (XR), where users are fully immersed in a computer-generated environment [[xr-ai-intersection]](../analyses/xr-ai-intersection.md).

## Key Characteristics
- **Immersive**: Users feel present within the virtual environment
- **Interactive**: Users can manipulate and interact with virtual elements
- **Computer-generated**: All content is digitally created rather than captured from reality

## Relationship to XR
VR is one of three main categories within Extended Reality:
1. **Virtual Reality (VR)**: Fully immersive, computer-generated environments
2. **Augmented Reality (AR)**: Digital overlays on real-world views
3. **Mixed Reality (MR)**: Blends virtual and physical objects in shared space [[reality-promises]](../concepts/reality-promises.md)

## AI Integration
The intersection of VR and AI is a significant research area, particularly in:
- User understanding through gaze tracking and behavior prediction [[xr-ai-topics]](../analyses/xr-ai-topics.md)
- Content generation for realistic virtual environments
- Intelligent virtual agents within VR spaces
