# Review Methodologies

## Definition
**Review methodologies** refer to structured approaches for synthesizing existing research literature to clarify definitions, identify gaps, and inform future research directions. In the context of Extended Reality (XR) and Artificial Intelligence (AI), these methodologies help map the emerging intersection field.

## Common Review Types
Based on established typologies [[typology-review]](../sources/typology-review.md) and review families [[review-family]](../sources/review-family.md):

### Systematic Reviews
- **Purpose**: Systematically search for, appraise, and synthesize research evidence
- **Characteristics**: Exhaustive searching, quality assessment, often adheres to guidelines
- **Use Case**: When comprehensive synthesis of existing evidence is needed

### Scoping Reviews
- **Purpose**: Clarify working definitions and conceptual boundaries of a topic or field
- **Characteristics**: Reconnaissance-level review, useful when literature is not yet comprehensively reviewed
- **Use Case**: Mapping the XR-AI intersection landscape [[XR-AI]](../sources/XR-AI.md)

### Narrative Reviews
- **Purpose**: Conventional literature review, particularly when contrasted with systematic approaches
- **Characteristics**: Flexible structure, may or may not include comprehensive searching
- **Use Case**: Providing overview of current state of knowledge

## Methodological Frameworks

### SALSA Framework
A four-component framework for analyzing review types:
1. **Search** - Completeness and comprehensiveness of literature search
2. **Appraisal** - Quality assessment of included studies
3. **Synthesis** - How findings are combined (narrative, tabular, graphical)
4. **Analysis** - Type of analysis performed (thematic, meta-analysis, descriptive)

### Joanna Briggs Institute Guidance
Provides structured approach for conducting systematic scoping reviews [[guidance-review]](../sources/guidance-review.md). Particularly useful when:
- Literature has not yet been comprehensively reviewed
- Topic exhibits complex or heterogeneous nature

## Review Families in Research
According to Sutton et al. (2026) [[review-family]](../sources/review-family.md), reviews are categorized into seven families:
1. Traditional Reviews (Critical, Integrative, Narrative)
2. Systematic Reviews
3. Review of Reviews (Overview, Umbrella)
4. Rapid Reviews
5. Qualitative Reviews
6. Mixed Method Reviews
7. Purpose Specific Reviews

## Applications in XR-AI Research
The CHI 2023 scoping review on XR and AI [[XR-AI]](../sources/XR-AI.md) demonstrates practical application:
- **Review Type**: Scoping review (not systematic)
- **Scope**: 311 papers from 203 international venues (2017-2021)
- **Purpose**: Map the intersection field and identify research gaps
- **Outcome**: Identified five main research topics and critical gaps

## Best Practices
From literature review writing guidance [[write-review]](../sources/write-review.md):

### Selection of Papers
- Be explicit about methodologies used for selection
- Report databases, search strings, and Boolean operators
- Use snowballing (forward/backward) if applicable
- Justify limitations regarding language, time frame, or geography

### Added Value
- Reviews must add value beyond simple overview
- Identify explicit research gaps
- Discuss methodological advantages/disadvantages
- Provide implications for future research
