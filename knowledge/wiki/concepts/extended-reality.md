# Extended Reality

## Definition
**Extended Reality (XR)** is an umbrella term encompassing all technologies that blend virtual and physical environments, ranging from fully immersive virtual worlds to augmented overlays on the real world [[xr-ai-intersection]](../analyses/xr-ai-intersection.md).

## XR Categories
XR includes three main categories:
1. **Virtual Reality (VR)**: Fully immersive, computer-generated environments where users are completely immersed [[concepts/virtual-reality]]
2. **Augmented Reality (AR)**: Digital overlays on real-world views, often with object-based interactions [[concepts/augmented-object-intelligence]]
3. **Mixed Reality (MR)**: Blends virtual and physical objects in shared space, sometimes using invisible robots for manipulation [[concepts/reality-promises]]

## XR-AI Intersection
The intersection of Extended Reality and Artificial Intelligence is an emerging research field combining these technologies to address fundamental questions in human-computer interaction, computer graphics, computer vision, and related fields [[xr-ai-intersection]](../analyses/xr-ai-intersection.md).

Five main research topics dominate this intersection:
- Using AI to create XR worlds (28.6%)
- Using AI to understand users (19.3%)
- Using AI to support interaction (15.4%)
- Investigating interaction with intelligent virtual agents (8.0%)
- Using XR to support AI research (2.3%)
