# Guidance for Conducting Systematic Scoping Reviews

## Publication Information
- **Title**: Guidance for conducting systematic scoping reviews
- **Source**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26134548/
- **Published**: 2011 (Joanna Briggs Institute)
- **Type**: Methodological guidance document

## Abstract Summary
This article introduces scoping reviews as a newer review type used for 'reconnaissance' - to clarify working definitions and conceptual boundaries of a topic or field. Scoping reviews are particularly useful when a body of literature has not yet been comprehensively reviewed, or exhibits a complex or heterogeneous nature not amenable to a more precise systematic review.

## Key Distinctions from Systematic Reviews
- **Purpose**: Clarify definitions and conceptual boundaries vs. answering specific research questions
- **Use Case**: When literature is not yet comprehensively reviewed or has complex/heterogeneous nature
- **Function**: Can determine value/scope of full systematic review OR stand alone to summarize findings, identify gaps, and make recommendations

## Methodology Guidance
Developed by members of the Joanna Briggs Institute and five Joanna Briggs Collaborating Centres. Provides structured approach for conducting systematic scoping reviews.
